Projects
- Ambiguity of Korean long-form negation polar questions and disambiguation by contextual evidence
-- Negative polar questions (NPQs) are widely known for encoding speaker's positive epistemic bias. A less well-known but established fact about Korean long-form NPQs (LN-PQs) is that the bias reading is polarity-ambiguous. I show the bias disambiguation by situating this construction in context, especially subjecting it to negative contextual evidence. I also employ evidentiality (grammatical encoding of the source of information) in Korean to highlight the negative bias reading in Korean LN-PQs.
-- I use [Gunlogson's (2008)](https://www.jbe-platform.com/content/journals/10.1075/bjl.22.06gun) commitment-based model to capture the two bias readings in Korean LN-PQs. Some typological considerations on Interrogative Flip (the flipping of evidential source in inquisitive speech acts) are also discussed.

- Form-based account of conjectural questions
-- A recent project that took off as a puzzle found in the interaction of sentence type and evidentiality in Korean LN-PQs, some sentential force and an utterance-final (rising) contour come to the foci of discussion. I aim to show that conjectural meaning in conjectural questions across languages (notably, German verb-final <i>wohl</i> questions in [Eckardt, 2020](https://semprag.org/index.php/sp/article/view/sp.13.9)) can be reduced to the interaction between declarative force, prosody AND evidential dimensions. 

- Context and inquisitively in Puerto Rican Spanish
-- I review the role of prosody in biased polar questions, introduced as Ladd's (1981) Outer vs. Inner negation questions in [Armstrong 2017](https://www.degruyterbrill.com/document/doi/10.1515/probus-2014-0016/html). Following up on Armstrong's DCT-style production study, I conducted a perception study on assertion contours of Puerto Rican Spanish (PRS), specifically under what contextual conditions can these contours be construed as purely speaker-biased.
