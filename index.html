About

Trilingual linguist, teacher, and reader of speech in context.

I am a South Korean linguist working across Korean and Spanish. My path through Spanish has included study, teaching, interpreting, translation, and linguistic research.

Since beginning my doctoral studies in 2021, I have been especially attentive to dialectal varieties of Spanish in the United States.

Research

Questions, meaning, and the social life of intonation.

My research interests include formal pragmatics, intonational meaning, Korean, Spanish, and biased polar questions.

Biased polar questions

How speakers design yes/no questions that carry expectations, stances, and conversational pressure.

Intonational meaning

How melody, contour, and prosodic choices contribute to interpretation beyond segmental words.

Formal pragmatics

How meaning emerges from linguistic form, context, common ground, and interactional commitments.

Korean and Spanish

Cross-linguistic attention to Korean and varieties of Spanish, especially in multilingual settings.
